Frequently Asked Questions

To get ranked, join our Discord server and participate in ranked tournaments. Our staff will evaluate your performance and assign you a tier based on your results. You can also request an evaluation in the #rank-request channel.
HT stands for "High Tier" and LT stands for "Low Tier". Each tier number (1–5) has a high and low subdivision, giving us 10 total tiers. HT1 is the highest possible rank, while LT5 is the entry level. Think of HT as the upper half and LT as the lower half of each tier bracket.
Yes! Tiers are updated weekly based on tournament performance. Win matches consistently and you'll climb the rankings. You can also request a tier review in our Discord if you believe your current tier doesn't reflect your actual skill level.
We currently rank players in seven game modes: Sword, Mace, Axe, UHC, Pot, Crystal, and SMP PvP. Your rank in each mode is tracked separately — you can be HT2 in Sword but LT4 in Crystal, for example.
If you believe your tier is inaccurate, open a ticket in our Discord server. Include evidence such as match recordings, tournament results, or clips showcasing your skill. Our staff will review your case and respond within 48 hours.
The tier list is updated weekly every Sunday evening. Major updates based on tournament results may be pushed mid-week as well. Follow our Discord announcements to stay up to date.
